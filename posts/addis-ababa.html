Addis Ababa: Coffee Spots, Museums, and Chill Corners

GoHabesha · Nov 28, 2025 · 7 min read

Addis has the strongest coffee culture on the planet. Mix that with museums, views, and local food scenes, and you've got a city that's way underrated.

Must-visit spots

  • National Museum — home to Lucy, the famous hominid fossil. A must-see for history buffs.
  • Entoto Hill — panoramic views of the entire city. Best at sunrise or sunset.
  • Mercato — one of Africa's largest open-air markets. Get ready to haggle and explore.
  • Holy Trinity Cathedral — beautiful architecture and final resting place of Emperor Haile Selassie.

Coffee culture

Don't skip a traditional coffee ceremony. It's a whole experience—roasting, grinding, and brewing right in front of you. Takes about 30-45 minutes but totally worth it. Visit Tomoca Coffee for the oldest coffee shop experience, or better yet, experience it in a local home.

Food tips

Try injera with wot. It's the national dish and super affordable. Most local restaurants serve massive portions for under 300 ETB. Don't miss tibs (grilled meat), kitfo (raw minced beef), and shiro (chickpea stew). For breakfast, try ful (fava beans) with fresh bread.

Getting around

The city is walkable in many areas, but taxis and minibuses are cheap and convenient. Blue taxis are shared and cost around 10-30 ETB per ride depending on the distance of the destination. Private taxis should be negotiated before getting in though.
