Lalibela: Exploring the Rock-Hewn Churches — A Complete Guide

GoHabesha · Oct 15, 2025 · 8 min read

Lalibela is home to 11 monolithic churches carved from solid rock. This UNESCO World Heritage site is one of Ethiopia's most sacred places.

The churches

The churches are divided into two groups. The Northern Group includes Bete Medhane Alem (the largest monolithic church in the world), Bete Maryam, and Bete Golgotha. The Eastern Group features Bete Amanuel and Bete Abba Libanos. Don't miss the iconic Bete Giyorgis (St. George's Church), carved in the shape of a cross.

Best time to visit

Dry season (October to March) is ideal with clear skies. Avoid the rainy season (June to September) when paths can be slippery. If you can, visit during Ethiopian Orthodox holidays like Timkat or Meskel for an unforgettable cultural experience, though expect larger crowds.

What to wear

This is a religious site, so dress modestly. Women should cover shoulders and wear skirts or pants below the knee. Men should avoid shorts. You'll need comfortable, sturdy shoes with good grip- you'll be walking on uneven stone surfaces and climbing narrow passages.

Practical tips

  • Hire a local guide—they provide invaluable context and help you navigate the complex.
  • Start early in the morning to avoid crowds and the midday heat.
  • Bring a headlamp or flashlight—some churches have dark, narrow tunnels.
  • Respect the priests and pilgrims—this is an active place of worship.
  • Altitude is around 2,500 meters—take it slow if you're not acclimated.
