Axum: Ancient Capital and the Ark of the Covenant

GoHabesha · Aug 10, 2025 · 6 min read

Ancient capital of the Aksumite Empire. See the towering obelisks (stelae), the Ark of the Covenant's reputed resting place, and ruins dating back 2,000 years.

The obelisks (stelae)

The most famous sight is the Axum Obelisk Park, home to massive granite stelae that served as grave markers. The largest standing obelisk is 24 meters tall, but the most impressive is the Great Stele (33 meters), which fell and broke into pieces. One obelisk was returned from Italy in 2005 after being looted during the Italian occupation.

St. Mary of Zion Church

This is where the Ark of the Covenant is said to be housed. The original church dates back to the 4th century, making it one of the oldest churches in Africa. Only one guardian is allowed to see the Ark, and no one else is permitted inside the chapel. The newer church next door is open to visitors.

Other historical sites

  • Queen of Sheba's Palace — ruins of what's believed to be the palace of the legendary queen.
  • King Ezana's Inscription — a trilingual stone tablet in Ge'ez, Greek, and Sabaean.
  • Tombs of Kings Kaleb and Gebre Meskel — underground burial chambers with impressive architecture.
  • Axum Museum — houses artifacts from the ancient kingdom, including crowns and coins.

Practical information

Axum is in the Tigray region, so you'll hear Tigrinya spoken alongside Amharic. The town is small and walkable. Fly from Addis Ababa (about 1.5 hours) or take a bus. The altitude is around 2,100 meters, so it's comfortable for most visitors. Dress modestly when visiting churches.
